Charity giving

A recent Econtalk podcast was with a founder of Give Directly. The idea of this is beautifully simple: money you give to the charity ends up directly in the hands of a very poor person. There are no NGOs involved. No goverment officials are involved. No millenium development goals are measured. The charity does not to bear the cost of a ridiculously overvalued official exchange rate. No tariffs or bribes need to be paid to deploy the funds. No presidential family members get contracts to build over-priced infrastructure. It’s just money paid via one of the several African digital wallets.

Simples!
